ADVANCED INERTIAL SYSTEMS

F = ma
Force = mass × acceleration
Rearranged: a = F/m
More mass = less acceleration (for same force)
MASS VS WEIGHT
Mass is constant everywhere (10 kg on Earth = 10 kg on Moon).
Weight changes with gravity (weight on Moon is less).
p = mv
Momentum = mass × velocity
Momentum is a VECTOR (has direction).
Units: kg⋅m/s
MOMENTUM CONSERVATION
Total momentum before = Total momentum after (if no external forces).
This is Newton's 1st Law restated.
f = μN
Friction force = coefficient of friction × normal force
μ (mu) = surface property
N = perpendicular force
STATIC VS KINETIC FRICTION
Static friction: Prevents motion from starting (higher μ).
Kinetic friction: Opposes ongoing motion (lower μ).
That's why starting motion needs more force.
Friction does NOT depend on contact area. Only on μ and N.
x_cm = (m₁x₁ + m₂x₂) / (m₁ + m₂)
Center of mass = weighted average position
System's center of mass moves as if all mass concentrated there.
INERTIAL REFERENCE FRAMES
Newton's 1st Law only works in inertial frames (non-accelerating).
In non-inertial frames, fictitious forces appear.
Example: Car accelerates → passenger feels "pushed back" (fictitious force).
Earth's surface is technically non-inertial (rotation), but effects are negligible for most applications.
KEY APPLICATIONS
• Vehicle collision analysis (momentum conservation)
• Brake systems (friction engineering)
• Rocket propulsion (momentum conservation in space)
• GPS systems (inertial frame corrections)
NEWTON'S 1ST LAW RESTATED
An object's momentum remains constant unless external forces act upon it.
