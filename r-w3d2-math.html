Formula Mechanics
‚è± 6 MINUTES
The slope-intercept form is represented as $$y = mx + b$$. 'm' represents the slope (rate of change/steepness). 'b' represents the y-intercept (starting point where x = 0).
The slope determines the direction and steepness of the line; the y-intercept anchors it to the y-axis.

Graphical Analysis
‚è± 6 MINUTES
Calculate slope from a graph using two distinct points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$. Use the formula: $$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$$
Slope is constant for any linear graph; the ratio of rise-over-run is identical regardless of the points chosen.
