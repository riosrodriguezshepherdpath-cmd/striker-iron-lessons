Newton's 1st Law - Quick Reference

NEWTON'S 1ST LAW
Things that are still stay still. Things that are moving keep moving. UNLESS a force makes them change.
INERTIA
How much something resists changing. Heavy things have more inertia. Light things have less inertia.
Example: A shopping cart full of groceries is harder to push than an empty cart. The full cart has more inertia.
MASS
How much "stuff" is in something. More mass = more inertia = harder to move.
Example: A bowling ball has more mass than a soccer ball. So it's harder to kick!
FRICTION
A force that slows things down. Happens when surfaces rub together.
Example: A ball rolls farther on smooth gym floor than on rough grass. Grass has more friction.
MOMENTUM
When something is moving and hard to stop. Fast + heavy = lots of momentum.
Example: When you sprint full speed, you can't stop right away. You have momentum!
BALANCED FORCES
When forces push equally in opposite directions. Nothing changes when forces are balanced.
Example: You're standing still. Gravity pulls you down. Ground pushes you up. Equal forces = no movement.
