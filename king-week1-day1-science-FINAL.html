Objects At Rest
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
Here's the first part of Newton's 1st Law:
üîë Objects at rest stay at rest
This means: Things that are still will STAY still.
Unless something makes them move!
EXAMPLE 1: A soccer ball sitting on the field. It won't move by itself. You have to KICK it to make it move.
EXAMPLE 2: A book on a table. It just sits there. It won't jump off the table by itself!
Why is this important? Because things don't move on their own.
You need a FORCE to make them move. A force is a push or a pull.

Objects In Motion
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
Here's the second part of Newton's 1st Law:
üîë Objects in motion stay in motion
This means: Things that are moving will KEEP moving.
Unless something stops them!
EXAMPLE 1: You kick a soccer ball. It rolls across the field. It keeps rolling until something stops it. Maybe friction from the grass. Or your teammate catching it.
EXAMPLE 2: You're riding your bike. You keep moving forward even after you stop pedaling! You keep going until you use the brakes.
In space, this is even more clear!
In space, there's no air. No friction. If you throw a ball in space, it keeps going FOREVER!
On Earth, things slow down because of friction and air resistance.

üî¨ EXPERIMENT: The Rolling Ball
