Method 1: Substitution
‚è± 8 MINUTES
Sir, use substitution when one variable is already isolated or easy to isolate.
1. Isolate one variable.
2. Substitute into the other equation.
3. Solve for the remaining variable.
EXAMPLE:
x + y = 10
x = 2y
Substitute 2y for x: (2y) + y = 10 -> 3y = 10 -> y = 10/3

Method 2: Elimination
‚è± 8 MINUTES
Sir, use elimination when variables are aligned.
1. Align variables (Ax + By = C).
2. Multiply to create opposing coefficients.
3. Add/Subtract equations to eliminate one variable.
EXAMPLE:
2x + y = 7
x - y = 2
Add equations: (2x+y) + (x-y) = 7+2 -> 3x = 9 -> x = 3
Elimination requires creating opposing coefficients for one variable.
