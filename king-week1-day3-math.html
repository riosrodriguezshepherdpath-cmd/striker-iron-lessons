Finding Common Denominators
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
To add or subtract fractions, the bottom numbers must be the same.
When bottom numbers are different, we need to find a common denominator.
Common denominator means: same bottom number.
üîë KEY POINT: Find a number that both bottom numbers can multiply into
Example: Add 1/2 + 1/4
Bottom numbers are 2 and 4. What number works for both? 4!
1/2 = 2/4
Now both have 4 on bottom!
Now we can add: 2/4 + 1/4 = 3/4
‚ùì QUESTION: What common denominator would you use for 1/3 and 1/6?

Subtracting Fractions
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
Subtracting fractions is just like adding. But you subtract the top numbers instead.
Rule: Bottom numbers must be the same!
üîë STEP 1: Make sure bottom numbers match
üîë STEP 2: Subtract the top numbers
üîë STEP 3: Keep the bottom number the same
5/8 - 2/8 = 3/8
Subtract tops: 5 - 2 = 3
Keep bottom: 8
Soccer example: You play 7/10 of the season. Your friend plays 4/10. How much more did you play?
7/10 - 4/10 = 3/10 more!
‚ùì QUESTION: What is 6/9 - 2/9?

Subtracting with Different Bottoms
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
What if the bottom numbers are different? First make them the same!
Example: 3/4 - 1/2
Step 1: Find common denominator. Use 4.
Step 2: Change 1/2 to 2/4 (multiply by 2/2)
3/4 - 2/4 = 1/4
Now we can subtract!
üîë REMEMBER: First make bottoms match, then subtract tops
Soccer example: You scored in 3/4 of your games. Your teammate scored in 1/2 of theirs. Who scored more often?
3/4 - 1/2 = 3/4 - 2/4 = 1/4 more!
