Week 2 Day 2: Checks and Balances

"What's up, striker! Yesterday we learned about the three branches. Today, we learn how they stop each other from getting too powerful! It's called Checks and Balances."
PHASE 1: What is a Check?

A "check" is when one branch restricts the power of another branch. It's like a defender stopping a striker!

üîë KEY POINT: Checks prevent one branch from becoming too strong.
PHASE 2: What is a Balance?

A "balance" means the powers are divided equally among the branches.

‚öΩ SOCCER TIP: A balanced team has strong defenders, midfielders, and forwards. Not just strikers!
PHASE 3: Examples of Checks

1. President can veto a law. 2. Congress can override a veto. 3. Supreme Court can declare a law unconstitutional.

PHASE 4: Keeping it Fair

Checks and Balances ensure the government works for the people, not just for itself.

PHASE 5: Physical Mission

Play a 1-on-1 game. When you are on defense, you "check" the offensive player. When you are on offense, you try to "balance" the play by shooting. Do this for 10 minutes.

üìñ BIBLE CONNECTION: "Iron sharpens iron, and one man sharpens another." (Proverbs 27:17). Branches check each other to make the government better!
