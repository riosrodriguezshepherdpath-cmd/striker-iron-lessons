Week 3 Day 2: Civics - Congress

Coach's Script: What's up striker! Today we are learning about Congress. This is the part of government that makes the rules (laws) for the whole country! Think of them as the coaches who write the rulebook for the game. Let's learn how they work!

Phase 1: Making the Rules

The Legislative Branch makes laws.

This branch is called Congress.

Congress is made up of two parts: The Senate and The House of Representatives.

Phase 2: The Senate (Equal Teams)

There are 100 Senators total.

Each state gets 2 Senators, no matter how big or small it is.

This keeps things fair for smaller states!

Phase 3: The House of Representatives (Population Teams)

The number of Representatives depends on how many people live in the state.

More people = More Representatives!

This keeps things fair based on population!

  • I know Representatives depend on population.
  • I know more people means more reps.

Phase 4: How a Bill Becomes a Law

First, an idea is written down (a Bill).

Both the Senate and the House must vote "YES".

If they agree, it goes to the President to sign!

  • I know a Bill is a new rule idea.
  • I know both parts must say "YES".
