Preamble Analysis
‚è± 6 MINUTES
The Preamble is the introduction to the Constitution. It sets the stage by explaining *why* the Constitution was written and what the government aims to achieve.
The Preamble does not define government powers, but rather the purpose and goals of the government itself.

"We the People" Power
‚è± 6 MINUTES
The phrase "We the People" is crucial. It signifies that the power of the government comes from the citizens, not from a monarch or a small group of leaders. This is known as popular sovereignty.
Citizens hold ultimate power and entrust it to the government through representatives.

Historical Comparison
‚è± 5 MINUTES
Under the Articles of Confederation, justice was inconsistently applied between states. The Constitution's goal to "establish Justice" created a unified, federal court system to ensure fairness nationwide.
HISTORICAL CONTEXT: The failure of the Articles to manage interstate disputes highlighted the need for a stronger central justice system.
