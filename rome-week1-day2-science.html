Inertial Reference Frames
‚è± 7 MINUTES
Newton's First Law only holds in inertial reference frames‚Äîcoordinate systems that are not accelerating.
In non-inertial (accelerating) frames, fictitious forces appear to act without physical source.
Earth's surface is technically non-inertial due to rotation, but effects are negligible for most applications. This approximation enables terrestrial physics without relativistic corrections.
Example: In accelerating car, passengers feel pushed backward. No actual backward force exists‚Äîit's inertia resisting forward acceleration. From passenger's non-inertial frame, a fictitious force appears. From external inertial frame, passengers simply resist acceleration per Newton's First Law.
Rotating reference frames produce centrifugal and Coriolis fictitious forces. These aren't "real" forces in inertial frames but must be included in equations of motion within rotating frames.
Advanced application: GPS satellite navigation requires inertial frame corrections. Satellite orbital motion creates non-inertial effects requiring relativistic adjustments for centimeter-level positioning accuracy.
