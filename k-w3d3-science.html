Week 3 Day 3: Science - Friction

Coach's Script: What's up striker! Ever wonder why a ball eventually stops rolling? That's friction! Today we are learning how surfaces affect how fast the ball moves. Let's make some friction!

Phase 1: What is Friction?

Friction is a force that acts against motion.

It happens when two things rub together.

Friction turns kinetic energy (moving) into heat energy (not moving).

Phase 2: Rough vs. Smooth Surfaces

Rough surfaces (like grass) have a lot of friction.

Smooth surfaces (like ice) have very little friction.

A ball stops faster on grass than on a gym floor.

  • I know grass has high friction.
  • I know smooth floors have low friction.

Phase 3: Cleats and Traction

Soccer players wear cleats to increase friction (traction).

Cleats help you run on grass without slipping.

Without friction, you couldn't stop or change direction!

  • I know cleats increase friction.
  • I know friction helps me stop.
