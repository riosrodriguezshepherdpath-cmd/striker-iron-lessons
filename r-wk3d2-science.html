Newton's Third Law
‚è± 7 MINUTES
Newton's Third Law states: "For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction." Forces always occur in pairs. Action and reaction forces act on *different* objects.
Action-reaction pairs do not cancel each other out because they act on different bodies.

Friction Interaction
‚è± 8 MINUTES
When walking, your foot exerts a backward force on the ground (Action). The ground exerts an equal and forward force on your foot (Reaction).
TACTICAL ANALYSIS: Why does the Earth not move? Because $m_{earth}$ is massive, making the acceleration $a = F/m_{earth}$ virtually zero, while your acceleration is significant.

Mass & Acceleration
‚è± 6 MINUTES
Equal force $\neq$ Equal acceleration. According to $F = ma$, for the same force, a smaller mass will have a larger acceleration, and a larger mass will have a smaller acceleration.
TACTICAL EXAMPLE: A gun recoiling ($m_{gun}a_{gun}$) vs. a bullet flying ($m_{bullet}a_{bullet}$).
