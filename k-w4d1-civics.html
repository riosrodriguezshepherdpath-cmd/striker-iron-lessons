Week 4 Day 1: Civics - The Judicial Branch

Coach's Script: What's up striker! We've learned about Congress making laws and the President enforcing them. Now, we are learning about the third branch: The Judicial Branch! This team interprets the laws to decide what they mean and if they are fair. Think of them as the referees on the field!

Phase 1: Interpreting the Rules

The Judicial Branch interprets laws.

This branch is made up of all the courts in the US.

They decide if a law agrees with the Constitution.

Phase 2: The Supreme Court

The highest court in the United States is the Supreme Court.

There are 9 Justices on the Supreme Court.

They make final decisions on very important cases.

  • I know the Supreme Court is the highest court.
  • I know there are 9 Justices.

Phase 3: Checks and Balances

The Judicial Branch can check Congress and the President.

If a law is unfair, the Supreme Court can declare it unconstitutional.

This ensures no branch becomes too powerful!

  • I understand the Court can stop unfair laws.
  • I know this keeps the government balanced.

Phase 4: Mental Check

Do the Courts make the rules or interpret them?

Phase 5: Physical Mission - Supreme Court Decision Mission

Set up a "Court" area with 3 chairs.

Have someone give you a scenario (e.g., "A rule says you can't kick the ball to the left").

Run to the court and decide if it is fair or unfair.

If you say "Fair", do 5 jumping jacks. If "Unfair", do 5 burpees!
