Defining Equivalence
‚è± 6 MINUTES
Equivalent ratios are ratios that have the same value when simplified. They are found by multiplying or dividing both terms of a ratio by the same non-zero number.
If you multiply or divide both sides of a ratio by the same number, the relationship remains unchanged.

Scaling Ratios
‚è± 6 MINUTES
Scale ratios up (multiply) to increase quantities, or scale down (divide) to decrease them while maintaining proportion.
TACTICAL EXAMPLE: Ratio of 3:2. Multiply by 2 = 6:4. Multiply by 5 = 15:10. All are equivalent to 3:2.
