Phase 1: The F=ma Formula

Force equals mass times acceleration.

Force (F) is the push or pull on the ball.

Mass (m) is how heavy the ball is.

Acceleration (a) is how fast the ball speeds up.

Phase 3: Mass Matters

If the ball is heavy (high mass), it takes more force to speed it up.

If the ball is light (low mass), it takes less force.

It is harder to accelerate a bowling ball than a soccer ball!

  • I understand Mass affects speed.
  • I can compare different ball weights.
  • I am focused on the goal.

Phase 4: Acceleration Action

Acceleration is the change in speed or direction.

A fast pass has high acceleration.

A slow roll has low acceleration.

  • I know acceleration is changing speed.
