Week 2 Day 4: Civics - Keeping it Fair

Coach's Script: What's up striker! Today we are learning how the government keeps things fair. Think of it like this: If one player makes all the rules, it's not a fair game! We need checks to make sure no one cheats.

Phase 1: Checks and Balances

This is a rule to keep power shared.

It means each team in government can stop another team.

This stops one team from cheating or getting too strong!

Phase 3: Courts vs. President

The President must do things by the rules.

The Supreme Court (judges) checks if the President followed the rules.

If not, the Court says "Stop!"

Phase 4: Congress vs. Courts

The Judges make big decisions (referee calls).

But Congress chooses the judges.

Congress can also change the rules (laws) if they don't like the decision.

  • I know Congress picks the judges.
  • I know Congress can change the rulebook.
