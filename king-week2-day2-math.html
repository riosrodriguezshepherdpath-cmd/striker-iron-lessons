Week 2 Day 2: Advanced Fractions

"What's up, striker! Yesterday we added simple fractions. Today, we learn to add fractions with different denominators! It's like playing different positions at the same time!"
PHASE 1: Common Denominator

You cannot add fractions with different denominators. You must find a "Common Denominator" first!

üîë KEY POINT: Denominators must be the same to add!
PHASE 2: The Math Steps

1. Find the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of the denominators. 2. Change the fractions. 3. Add!

‚öΩ MATH TIP: For 1/2 + 1/4, the common denominator is 4! 1/2 becomes 2/4.
PHASE 3: Soccer Example

You practiced 1/3 of an hour in the morning and 1/2 of an hour in the afternoon. How much total? (LCM is 6: 2/6 + 3/6 = 5/6 hour!)

PHASE 4: Practice Problems

PHASE 5: Physical Mission

Use a stopwatch. Dribble for 1/2 of a minute (30 sec) with your left foot, then 1/4 of a minute (15 sec) with your right foot. Add the fractions to find the total time spent dribbling (3/4 of a minute!).
