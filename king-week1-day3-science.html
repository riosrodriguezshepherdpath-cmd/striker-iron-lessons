Experiment 1: The Coin Drop
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
Time for a fun experiment! You'll need: a cup, an index card, and a coin.

üî¨ THE COIN DROP EXPERIMENT:

Step 1: Put the cup on a table.
Step 2: Put the index card on top of the cup.
Step 3: Put the coin on top of the card. Center it over the cup.
Step 4: Quickly flick the card away sideways. What happens to the coin?
What happened: The coin dropped straight into the cup!
Why: The coin was at rest. When you flicked the card, the coin wanted to stay at rest. So it dropped straight down!
üîë KEY POINT: Objects at rest want to stay at rest (inertia!)
‚ùì QUESTION: What would happen if you moved the card SLOWLY instead of flicking it fast?

Experiment 2: The Moving Marble
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
Another experiment! You'll need: a toy car (or skateboard) and a marble (or small ball).

üî¨ THE MOVING MARBLE EXPERIMENT:

Step 1: Put the marble on top of the toy car.
Step 2: Push the car forward quickly. What happens to the marble?
Step 3: Now get the car moving at a steady speed. Put the marble on top while it's moving.
Step 4: Stop the car suddenly. What happens to the marble now?
What happened: The marble rolled backward when you started. It rolled forward when you stopped!
Why: The marble wanted to stay still when you started moving. It wanted to keep moving when you stopped!
üîë KEY POINT: This is exactly what happens in a car when you speed up or brake!
