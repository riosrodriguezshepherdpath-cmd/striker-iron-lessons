Fraction Definition & Structure
‚è± 5 MINUTES
A fraction represents part of a whole. It quantifies incomplete sets.
3/8
This is fractional notation.
Two components: numerator and denominator.
Fractions are division operators. The bar represents division.
Structure analysis: Top number over bottom number.
TACTICAL EXAMPLE: Unit has 8 missions assigned. Unit completes 3 successfully. Success rate expressed as fraction: 3/8.
The fraction bar means "out of" or "divided by."
3/8 translates to: "3 out of 8" or "3 divided by 8."

Numerator Analysis
‚è± 5 MINUTES
The numerator is the top number. It quantifies parts obtained.
5/10
Numerator: 5
Represents quantity achieved or possessed.
Numerator indicates success quantity in ratio analysis.
RESOURCE ALLOCATION: Supply depot contains 10 crates. Your squad requisitions 5 crates. Numerator: 5. Your squad obtained 5 units.
MISSION SUCCESS: Battalion executes 10 operations. Battalion achieves 7 objectives. Numerator: 7. Success count.
The numerator cannot exceed the denominator in proper fractions.
You cannot obtain 10 crates from an 8-crate depot. Improper fraction.
