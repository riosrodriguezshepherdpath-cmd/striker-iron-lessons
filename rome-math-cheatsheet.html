FRACTION OPERATIONS

FRACTIONS AS DIVISION
The fraction bar IS the division operator.
3/4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75
Fractions and decimals are interchangeable.
EQUIVALENT FRACTIONS
a/b = (a×n)/(b×n)
Multiply both numerator and denominator by same value.
Example: 2/3 = 4/6 = 8/12
Multiplying by n/n is multiplying by 1, which doesn't change the value.
COMMON DENOMINATORS
Required for addition/subtraction
Find LCD (Least Common Denominator)
Convert fractions to equivalent forms with same denominator
Example: 1/3 + 1/4 → 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12
ADDITION (Same Denominator)
a/c + b/c = (a+b)/c
Add numerators, keep denominator
Example: 2/5 + 3/5 = 5/5 = 1
MULTIPLICATION
(a/b) × (c/d) = (a×c)/(b×d)
Multiply numerators together
Multiply denominators together
Example: 2/3 × 3/5 = 6/15 = 2/5
Multiplying two proper fractions (< 1) produces a SMALLER result. This is scaling, not traditional multiplication.
DIVISION (Reciprocal Multiplication)
(a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) × (d/c)
Flip the second fraction (reciprocal)
Then multiply
Example: 3/4 ÷ 1/2 = 3/4 × 2/1 = 6/4 = 3/2
WHY INVERT AND MULTIPLY?
Division asks "how many fit?"
3/4 ÷ 1/2 means "how many halves fit in 3/4?"
Answer: 1.5 halves (which is 3/2)
SIMPLIFYING FRACTIONS
Divide both numerator and denominator by their GCF (Greatest Common Factor).
Example: 6/8 → divide both by 2 → 3/4
KEY PRINCIPLE
Fractions represent proportional relationships.
Mathematical truth remains constant regardless of representation.
1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 = 0.5 = 50%
