Week 3, Day 1: Roman Applied Science & Engineering

1. Engineering Marvels

The Romans were masters of applied science, focusing on functional infrastructure.

  • Roman Concrete (Opus Caementicium): A mixture of volcanic ash (pozzolana), lime, and water that could set underwater.
  • The Arch and Vault: Allowed for the distribution of massive weight, enabling the construction of the Colosseum.

2. Water Management (Hydraulics)

They developed complex systems to bring fresh water into cities.

  • Aqueducts: Structures using gravity to move water over long distances with a precise gradient.
  • Siphons: Pressurized pipes used to transport water across valleys when bridges weren't feasible.

3. Medicine and Public Health

Romans prioritized sanitation to maintain large urban populations.

  • Sanitation Systems: Construction of complex sewers, such as the Cloaca Maxima.
  • Military Medicine: Advanced battlefield surgery techniques, including tourniquets and specialized instruments.
