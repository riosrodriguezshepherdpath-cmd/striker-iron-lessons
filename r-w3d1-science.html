Law Definition
‚è± 6 MINUTES
Newton's Third Law states every action force has an equal, opposite reaction force. Forces always occur in pairs, acting on different objects simultaneously. These forces are equal in magnitude but opposite in direction.
Forces cannot exist in isolation; they are interactions between two objects.
TACTICAL EXAMPLE: A soldier pushing against a wall experiences equal force back.

Ballistic Applications
‚è± 6 MINUTES
Gunfire demonstrates the law perfectly through the principle of recoil. The gun pushes the bullet forward, while the bullet pushes the gun backward. The gun's mass is greater, resulting in less acceleration than the bullet.
TACTICAL EXAMPLE: Artillery firing requires stabilizing stabilizers to counteract the backward force.

Vehicle Dynamics
‚è± 6 MINUTES
Rocket propulsion relies on expelling mass rapidly in one direction. The rocket pushes gas backward; the gas pushes the rocket forward. This allows maneuverability and acceleration even in the vacuum of space.
Thrust is generated by accelerating mass in the opposite direction of travel.
