Week 3 Day 1: Science - Speed and Velocity

Coach's Script: What's up striker! Today we are learning how to measure how fast you are moving and in what direction! Knowing the difference between speed and velocity helps you become a smarter, faster player. Let's get moving!

Phase 1: What is Speed?

Speed is how fast an object moves.

It only tells you how much distance you cover in a certain time.

Example: The ball is moving at 30 miles per hour.

üí° SIMPLE IDEA: Speed = Fastness (How fast?)

  • I know it doesn't tell me direction.

Phase 2: What is Velocity?

Velocity is speed AND direction.

It tells you how fast you are going, and where you are going.

Example: The ball is moving at 30 miles per hour toward the goal.

üí° SIMPLE IDEA: Velocity = Fastness + Direction (How fast + Where?)
  • I know velocity needs speed and direction.
  • I know direction can be North, South, Left, Right.

Phase 3: The Difference

If you run in a circle at a steady pace, your speed stays the same.

But, your velocity changes constantly because your direction is constantly changing!

  • I know velocity changes if direction changes.
  • I know speed is just a number.

Phase 4: Mental Check

Is "5 meters per second" a speed or a velocity?

  • I know it's speed (missing direction).
  • I am ready to move!

Phase 5: Physical Mission - Speed vs. Velocity Mission

Sprint from one spot to another as fast as you can. Time yourself (Speed).

Sprint the same distance, but shout the direction you are going (Velocity). Example: "Sprinting North!"

Which one was more important for getting the ball?
