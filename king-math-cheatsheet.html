Fractions - Quick Reference

WHAT IS A FRACTION?
A fraction shows part of a whole. It has two numbers: top and bottom.
3/8
3 = Top number (numerator) = Parts you have
8 = Bottom number (denominator) = Total parts
Example: Pizza cut into 8 slices. You eat 3 slices. You ate 3/8 of the pizza.
NUMERATOR (Top Number)
The number on TOP. It tells you how many parts you have.
DENOMINATOR (Bottom Number)
The number on BOTTOM. It tells you how many equal parts in the whole.
COMPARING FRACTIONS
When bottom numbers are the SAME, look at top numbers. Bigger top number = bigger fraction.
Example: 7/10 is bigger than 5/10 (because 7 is bigger than 5)
ADDING FRACTIONS (Same Bottom)
When bottom numbers are the SAME: Add the top numbers. Keep the bottom number the same.
2/8 + 3/8 = 5/8
Add tops: 2 + 3 = 5
Keep bottom: 8
Example: You run 2/8 of a mile. Then you run 3/8 more. Total = 5/8 of a mile.
