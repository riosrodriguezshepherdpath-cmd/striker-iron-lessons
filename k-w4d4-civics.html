Week 4 Day 4: Civics - How Courts Work

Coach's Script: What's up striker! You know the Supreme Court is the top referee, but how do they get cases? Cases start at the bottom and work their way up, like a tournament! Let's learn how a case moves from a local trial to the national stage.

Phase 1: The Trial Court (The First Match)

All cases start in a Trial Court.

This is where evidence is shown, witnesses talk, and a judge or jury decides who wins.

Think of this as the first round of the playoffs!

Phase 2: Appellate Courts (The Rematch)

If you lose in trial court, you can appeal to an Appellate Court.

They don't look at new evidence; they just check if the first judge followed the rules correctly.

  • I know what to "appeal" means.

Phase 3: Supreme Court (The Championship)

If you lose in the appellate court, you can ask the Supreme Court to hear your case.

They only take cases that affect the whole country or the Constitution.

  • I know the Supreme Court is the final step.
  • I know they select important cases.
