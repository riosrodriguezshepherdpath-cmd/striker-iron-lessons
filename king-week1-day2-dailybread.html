Week 1 Day 2: The Heart of a Worker

COACH'S SCRIPT:

Alright striker, listen up. Today we're learning that nothing moves unless you make it move, and nothing stops unless something stops it. That ball sitting on the field? It's waiting for you to put force into it. Your effort? It doesn't happen unless you decide to push. Newton figured out that the universe follows rules, and so does your training. You want to score goals? You've got to be the force that makes it happen. God gave you a body that can run and kick and push, so use it with everything you've got. Get out there and be the force that changes the game. Now move!
Phase 1: Working for the Right Coach
Every time you practice, every time you study, every time you help someone, you're not just working for your parents or teachers. You're working for God, and He's the best coach you could ever have because He sees every effort you make, even the ones nobody else notices.
‚ùì QUESTION: Who should you really be working for when you do your chores and homework?
Phase 2: No Shortcuts on the Field
A lazy player who skips practice and takes shortcuts will never be ready when game day comes. God wants workers who show up every day, do the hard work, and don't look for the easy way out because that's how champions are built.
‚ùì QUESTION: What happens to a soccer player who skips practice and only shows up on game day?
Phase 3: The Worker's Reward
When you work hard and do your best, you're building something that lasts longer than a trophy or a good grade. You're building character, and character is what makes you someone people can trust and depend on, just like a team captain everyone respects.
‚ùì QUESTION: What are you building when you work hard every day, even when it's difficult?
Phase 4: Complaining Versus Competing
Complainers waste energy talking about how hard things are instead of using that energy to get the job done. Competitors see hard work as training that makes them stronger, and they attack it with a smile because they know it's making them better.
‚ùì QUESTION: What's the difference between a complainer and a competitor when facing hard work?
Phase 5: Your Daily Assignment
God gives you specific work to do every single day, and part of growing up is learning to see that work as your mission. Whether it's cleaning your room, helping your brother, or finishing your math, that's your training ground for the bigger missions God has planned for your future.
‚ùì QUESTION: Why does God give you small jobs like chores and homework now?
