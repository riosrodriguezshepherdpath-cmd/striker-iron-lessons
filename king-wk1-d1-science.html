What is Inertia?

Inertia means objects like to keep doing what they are doing. Objects at rest want to stay at rest. Objects in motion want to stay in motion. They are "lazy" and hate change!

Objects at Rest
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
An object at rest has zero velocity. It needs an "unbalanced force" to start moving. Think of a kickoff. The ball is at rest. Your foot is the force.

Objects in Motion
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
Once moving, an object wants to go in a straight line. It would never stop in outer space! On Earth, other forces make it stop. Friction and gravity are those forces.
üîë KEY POINT: Moving objects stay moving unless stopped.

Force and Friction
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
Friction happens when two things rub together. The grass rubs against the soccer ball. This force acts against the ball's inertia. This is why the ball slows down.
SOCCER EXAMPLE: Tall grass has more friction than a smooth turf pitch.
